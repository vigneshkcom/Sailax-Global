Why switch

What a residential heat pump
means for your home

A heat pump is not just a heating upgrade. It's a smarter, cleaner, and more cost-effective way to run your home — now and long into the future.

💷

Lower Running Costs

Heat pumps produce 3–4 units of heat for every unit of electricity consumed. That efficiency advantage translates into meaningfully lower energy bills compared to gas or oil systems.

🌿

Cleaner Heating

No direct emissions from your heating system. As the electricity grid decarbonises, your heat pump gets cleaner over time — without any action required on your part.

🌡️

Consistent Comfort

Even, steady warmth throughout your home — no cold spots, no temperature swings, no noise. Reliable performance across all UK seasons.

🔮

Future-Ready Heating

Gas boilers are being phased out. Installing a heat pump now puts your home ahead of incoming UK regulations — and means you switch on your own terms, not under pressure.

⛽

Freedom from Fuel Prices

Especially valuable for oil and LPG households. No more volatile fuel costs, no delivery logistics, no dependency on commodity prices to heat your home through winter.

🏡

Added Property Value

An improved EPC rating makes your home more attractive to future buyers. A modern heat pump installation is increasingly viewed as a meaningful and desirable upgrade.

The Boiler Upgrade Scheme (BUS) is a UK government programme providing grants to homeowners who replace fossil fuel heating systems with low-carbon alternatives such as heat pumps. The grant is applied directly by the installer — reducing your upfront cost. You don't receive the money yourself; it comes off the total installation price.
Eligibility depends on several factors: you need to own the property, have a valid Energy Performance Certificate, and be replacing a fossil fuel heating system. When you enquire with Sailax, we work through your eligibility in plain English during our initial consultation — with no obligation to proceed.
The level of BUS grant support depends on what you're replacing. The higher amount — up to £9,000 — is specifically for homes currently heated by oil or LPG. Properties replacing gas or other systems may qualify for a different level of support. Sailax will confirm exactly what applies to your situation during your initial consultation.
