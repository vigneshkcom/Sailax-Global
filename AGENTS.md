# Sailax Marketing Operating System

## Mission
- Grow qualified homeowner enquiries for Sailax's UK residential heat pump offer.
- Prioritize lead quality and conversion rate over raw traffic volume.

## Global Success Metrics
- Primary KPI: form submissions per unique session.
- Secondary KPIs: call clicks, qualified-lead rate, cost per qualified lead (when paid traffic exists).

## Non-Negotiables
- Keep claims factual and supportable.
- Avoid absolute guarantees, fabricated urgency, or unverifiable social proof.
- Preserve a no-pressure tone and plain-English messaging.
- Maintain a single clear primary CTA per section.

## Voice & Messaging Standards
- Write in clear UK English.
- Lead with outcome, then mechanism, then proof.
- Keep headlines concise and specific.
- Prefer numbers and concrete detail over vague adjectives.

## CRO Workflow
1. Diagnose one bottleneck (clarity, trust, friction, or offer framing).
2. Propose no more than three changes per test cycle.
3. Define expected impact and measurable success criteria before implementation.
4. Instrument events before concluding whether a change worked.
5. Log every experiment in `templates/experiment-brief.md` format.

## Implementation Rules
- Keep landing page changes accessible (semantic HTML, visible focus states, mobile-first checks).
- Preserve page speed by minimizing third-party scripts.
- Track every primary CTA and form step with analytics events.

## Handoff Expectations
- For each change set, provide:
  - what changed,
  - why it should improve conversion,
  - what to measure,
  - a rollback plan.
