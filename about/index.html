The Technology

How an air source heat pump heats your home

A heat pump doesn't burn fuel — it moves heat. It extracts warmth already present in the outside air and upgrades it to a useful temperature using a refrigerant and compressor, then circulates it through your home.

1

Outside air drawn in

The outdoor unit draws in outside air — even at temperatures as low as −15°C — and extracts the heat energy held within it using a refrigerant.

2

Refrigerant compressed

The refrigerant is compressed, which significantly raises its temperature — the same principle as how a bicycle pump heats up when you pump it fast.

3

Heat transferred to water

The heat passes through a heat exchanger into the water in your home's central heating circuit, raising it to your required flow temperature.

4

Warm water circulated

Heated water flows through your radiators or underfloor heating, and is stored in a hot water cylinder for taps and showers throughout the home.
